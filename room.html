Our Spots

Tourist Spot

Known for its dramatic limestone rock formations, pristine white-sand beaches, and turquoise waters, often dubbed a "mystical" hidden gem.

Mount Redondo

Mount Redondo, is best known for its unique 100-hectare Natural Bonsai Forest. It is the highest peak on the island, featuring century-old stunted trees including narra and molave due to harsh, rocky terrain and strong winds.

Bue Lagoon

A stunning, secluded natural pool in Libjo, framed by lush limestone cliffs and emerald-green forests. Known for its calm, vibrant blue-green, shallow waters, it is ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and, often, finding solitude.

Hagakhak Island

Renowned for the spectacular Hagakhak Rock Formations, a, natural, million-of-years old, sculpture, carved by wind and sea. It features striking, layered, sedimentary, rocks, dubbed a, coastal, masterpiece.

Lake Labubu

A rare meromictic lake featuring distinct, unmixed layers of freshwater on top and denser saltwater below. Surrounded by limestone cliffs and forests, this, along with its 650-meter underwater cave system (the longest in the Philippines).

Cabacongan Beach

A serene, secluded paradise in Columbus, Basilisa, featuring soft white sand, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and dramatic, towering limestone cliffs.

PBMA Shrine

A sacred, meticulously maintained site in Barangay Aurelio, San Jose, Dinagat Islands, dedicated to Ruben Edera Ecleo Sr., founder of the Philippine Benevolent Missionaries Association (PBMA).
