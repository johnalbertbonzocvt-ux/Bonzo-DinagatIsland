About Us

The Dinagat Islands, located in the Caraga region of the Philippines, is an emerging, mystical province known for its untouched natural beauty, featuring dramatic limestone formations, white sand beaches, and deep blue lagoons. Accessible by ferry from Surigao City, this island province offers a peaceful alternative to nearby Siargao, with highlights including the Blue Lagoon in Libjo, Lake Bababu, and Mount Redondo

Tourist Spot

Known for its dramatic limestone rock formations, pristine white-sand beaches, and turquoise waters, often dubbed a "mystical" hidden gem.

Mount Redondo

Mount Redondo, is best known for its unique 100-hectare Natural Bonsai Forest. It is the highest peak on the island, featuring century-old stunted trees including narra and molave due to harsh, rocky terrain and strong winds.

Bue Lagoon

A stunning, secluded natural pool in Libjo, framed by lush limestone cliffs and emerald-green forests. Known for its calm, vibrant blue-green, shallow waters, it is ideal for swimming, snorkeling, and, often, finding solitude.

Hagakhak Island

Renowned for the spectacular Hagakhak Rock Formations, a, natural, million-of-years old, sculpture, carved by wind and sea. It features striking, layered, sedimentary, rocks, dubbed a, coastal, masterpiece.

Lake Labubu

A rare meromictic lake featuring distinct, unmixed layers of freshwater on top and denser saltwater below. Surrounded by limestone cliffs and forests, this, along with its 650-meter underwater cave system (the longest in the Philippines).

Cabacongan Beach

A serene, secluded paradise in Columbus, Basilisa, featuring soft white sand, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and dramatic, towering limestone cliffs.

PBMA Shrine

A sacred, meticulously maintained site in Barangay Aurelio, San Jose, Dinagat Islands, dedicated to Ruben Edera Ecleo Sr., founder of the Philippine Benevolent Missionaries Association (PBMA).

Reviews

Noticeable Personalities Who Visited The Beautiful Island

Madelyn Cline

American actress and model

“Dinagat Island is one of the most underrated gems in the Philippines. The limestone cliffs and hidden lagoons feel like something out of a movie set, untouched and mystical. It’s not a place for luxury seekers there are few amenities but that’s exactly what makes it special. If you’re looking for raw beauty and a sense of adventure, Dinagat will leave you speechless.”

Timothée Chalamet

American-French actor

“Dinagat Island feels like stepping into a secret paradise. The beaches are quiet, the cliffs are dramatic, and the water is unbelievably clear. It’s not polished like Boracay or Palawan, but that rawness is exactly what makes it magical.”

Madison Beer

American singer-songwriter

“I didn’t expect Dinagat to be this enchanting. The island has a mystical energy you can sense it in the landscapes and the people. It’s a place for reflection and adventure, far away from the usual tourist trail.”
